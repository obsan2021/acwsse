History & Governance

From a single railway well to a resilient municipal utility.

The enterprise's history traces Adama's evolution from a regional railway settlement into a major administrative and industrial center — and a decades-long effort to solve one defining challenge: naturally occurring fluoride in the region's groundwater.

1982
National authority takeover
Local railway water assets transition to the national Water Supply and Sewerage Authority (WSSA), ending decades of fragmented management.
1983
Enterprise established
The Adama Water Supply and Sewerage Service Enterprise is formally established under the Oromia Regional Water, Mineral and Energy Bureau.
2002
Koka surface water plant
The first Koka–Awash River treatment plant is commissioned, addressing Rift Valley groundwater fluoride contamination via a 17 km conveyance line.
2016
342M Birr optimization
A system optimization raises Koka treatment capacity to 43,270 m³/day, briefly lifting municipal coverage to 90%.
2025
Mojo boreholes online
Boreholes 23 and 24 begin partial delivery, introducing 13,000 m³/day of groundwater into the Adama network.
2026
Full integration & SCADA
Complete project operationalization, a centralized solar-powered SCADA network, and the new G+5 headquarters go live.
Koka Dam on the Awash River — source of the enterprise's original surface water supply

The fluoride challenge

Rift Valley boreholes met volumetric demand but carried elevated natural fluoride, causing dental and skeletal fluorosis. Surface water from the Awash River, blended under strict WHO-guideline protocols, resolved the quality gap.

Engineering & Systems

A dual-source network, engineered for reliability.

Deep groundwater from the Mojo Well Field is blended with treated Awash River surface water, then moved through 244 kilometers of transmission and distribution pipeline to ten pressure zones across the city.

Mojo Well Field — geotechnical specifications

Thirteen deep production wells in the Anna Lume area, drilled with specialized techniques to prevent vertical contamination and ensure long-term aquifer stability.

Drilling diameter26″ at surface, narrowing to 12″ at target aquifer depth
Casing materialHigh-strength, corrosion-resistant copper-bearing steel blind casings
ScreensShuttered steel screens sized to local aquifer sand-gravel distribution
Isolation barriersPuddle clay sealing layers to prevent vertical cross-contamination
Surface completionReinforced concrete pads with sanitary seals
TestingSystematic test pumping for dynamic drawdown & discharge rate

Hydraulic Blending

Managing fluoride through mass-balance blending.

Low-fluoride Koka surface water is mixed with Mojo groundwater in a central reservoir, monitored by real-time chemical sampling and flow-control valves, to hold final fluoride concentration below 1.5 mg/L per 1993 WHO guidelines.
